ITERATIVE DESIGN: SOCKSOHO

SockSoho is a startup which aims to sell a variety of men's socks in India in unique and trendy designs and quality of cloth akin to Uniqlo.

We picked this startup because we liked the idea of making fashion more accessible in India and we truly loved the designs on the socks and wanted them to reach more people. Our primary goals for our iterative design were:

  • simplification of functionality

  • using minimalistic designs and color schemes

  • finding the right balance between differentiating the website from other shopping websites and ensuring that the features are recallable by avid online shoppers.

We tried to meet these goals, along with meeting Jeff's goals for high quality user experience: usability, learnability, memorability and efficiency, as we iteratively designed the interface of this webpage.

PROTOTYPES

Here is our high-fidelity mockup with all the interactive features we want to give to the real website. Before we finalized on this version of the mockup, we had a mockup with a slightly different shopping page and cart page. Our critique involved the following pieces of feedback:

  • Replace the question mark icon with the words "Help Center" so it can more accurately convey the gist of the content on the "Help Page."

  • Modify the checkout page to include the price of each item.

  • Replace the black cart icon for "add to cart" action with the words "Add to cart" and sizes "S", "M", "L."

  • Add scrollable list in the cart page.

  • Include stars as rating scale in "Reviews" page.

  • The cart page was unlike anything the critiques had seen before but they appreciated the uniqueness of it and believed that it would still be usable.

USER TESTING: TASKS, RESPONSES, ANALYSIS

  • Tasks:

    • Imagine you have a question about shipping logistics. How would you find help on the website?

    • Browse the shopping page, add 3 items to the shopping cart and checkout.

  • We also asked them general questions about their thoughts on the different pages, their impression of the start up, and how we could improve the mockups. Following are the highlights of the user responses:

    • There was a 100% success rate for task completion for both tasks.

    • All users felt that their first impression of the website was that it was an online store that sells high quality product with unique designs.

    • One user felt that the written content was too light, and they did not love the black frame. They also suggested changing the light blue background.

    • The users commented that there was no problem with navigation and that it had a smooth flow and was straightforward to get help and read reviews.

    • One user really liked the simplicity of adding to cart simply by clicking on size, making this action a 1-click process instead of a 2-click process of selecting, size, and then clicking "add to cart."

    • The tasks were uninamously declared by the users as very easy to fulfill by navigating through the pages on the prototype.

    • One user commented that they wished that they could see a number on the cart when they added items to the cart.

    • For the reviews page, we received feedback to add bigger pictures, more testimonies and identities of reviewers to improve legitimacy of reviews and build a sense of trust.

  • Analysis:

    • We had three participants aged 22-47: one male and two female, all of them browsing on Chrome. One was full-time employed, one was a student and the other was a stay-at-home mom. From this, we understand that all three users would be avid users of the internet and have potentially shopped online many times before.

    • Overall, our feedback tended to be more about design and less about functionality. For the former, we were recommended to make the website more professional, modern and adult-looking, while for the latter, we were lauded for the easy of navigation and usage.

    • The average time to complete task 1 (getting help about shipping) was 35 seconds. The average clicks were 8. We believe that while the time variable was efficient, the number of clicks could be reduced.

    • The average time to complete task 2 (adding 3 items to cart) was 89 seconds. The average clicks were 16. We thought we did great on time since three different socks of different sizes were successfully added and checked out in less than 90 seconds. The clicks could be reduced again but we would be more interested to make a comparison with the average clicks on other shopping websites before further analyzing and iterating on this design.

    CONCLUSION

    We learnt how important it is to have an iterative approach to the design development of user experiences. We understood that it is always helpful to get critique and feedback, both from professionals in the field, as well as the very people who will be the end users of the product, before we land on the final mockup for the product. Such feedback helps us ground our ideas and keep them customer-focused. Talking to each other and critically discussing the need for every click and every feature, and every color scheme helped us achieve our goals for a minimalistic design that is still robust on functionality. The iterative approach helped us save time and resources by ensuring that we don't deploy a product which is not fully tested. This cost effective approach helped us keep user experience as the first priority in the design process.
