Thank you for visiting my portfolio! I'm glad that you're here. This is a collection of my projects done as part of my coursework in UI-UX at Brown University. I hope you enjoy reading as much I enjoyed putting this together :)

PROJECTS

Following are some of the projects that I have created. The first three are UI-UX related projects which helped me learn core design concepts, principles of user experience formation, prototyping tools and user reaction evaluation. Throughout all my projects, I strived to maintain key usability principles such as smooth navigation, intuitive layout, and an engaging visual design.

Responsive Redesign

Completely redesigned and revamped the website of a local real estate company, and created a final product that is responsive, functional and easy to use for homeowners looking to sell/rent and people looking to find a home or office.

Iterative Design

Iteratively designed a website for a socks startup through sketches, low-fidelity wireframes, and high-fidelity prototypes. Tested the final prototype on end users via UserTesting to receive feedback.

React Development

Created a shopping website using React and implemented cross-filtering, price-sorting, and shopping cart functionality.

VR Telepresence

Proposed an idea for a real life usecase of Virtual Reality. Researched how to use VR for telepresence (virtual travel, remote participation) and presented my discovery and ideas in a slidedeck.

WHO AM I?

I am a senior year undergraduate student at Brown University concentrating in Computer Science and Economics. I am from India and have a passion for product management and human-centered design thinking. My interests lie in hiking, listening to world music, and exploring new cuisines.

During my time at Brown, I have taken courses in Virtual Reality, Cybersecuity, Game Theoretical Economics, International Relations, Contemporary Architecture, and South Asian studies. Having done an independent study in Data Driven Product Management, I have become very interested in learning about product cycles and the data culture in different organisations and different companies, and the various strategies and tools that can be used to optimize these processes.

I believe that it is just as important for a product manager to be well-versed with design principles and frameworks to work with UI-UX teams as they should be with computer science concepts to work with engineering teams. My coursework in UI-UX has augmented this learning by helping me gain an insight into persona building, storyboarding, experimentation, a/b testing, user surveying and iterative designing.
